## What it is
A civic-action web app for Madrid. Someone walking through their neighbourhood
sees a **QR code** on a sign, scans it, and is asked **one** question:

> **"Would you like more greenery in your area — trees, grass, flowers, bushes?"**
> *Why it matters: greenery lowers local temperature 2–4°C, cleans the air, and supports wellbeing.*

They tap **Yes** or **No**, get a thank-you, and are nudged to **share** the link
with a neighbour. If enough people say yes **before the deadline**, the idea is
pushed to that district's council and everyone who voted gets a "**your issue is
being heard**" notification.

## Why it exists (two goals)
- **For residents:** a frictionless way to pressure the local council to act.
- **For the creator (you):** collect district-level data on what neighbourhoods
  want and where support concentrates — to decide where to push hardest.

## Who it's for
Residents of Madrid (~5,000 to start), the creator/admin, and — later — council
members who respond to passed ideas.

---

## The core loop (Week-1 MVP)
```
Scan QR  →  Greenery question + benefit  →  Yes / No
        →  Thank you  →  Share with a friend
        →  (optional) leave private feedback
        →  if YES-votes ≥ threshold by deadline:
              status = passed → notify voters → idea sent to council
```

## What's in vs. out

| In (Week 1) | Out (Phase 2) |
|---|---|
| QR → single greenery question + benefit text | GoFundMe (external link, post-threshold, refund if unmet) |
| Yes/No vote, one per device | Council conversation published as a public "PR story" |
| Thank-you + share link | Multiple questions/categories per district |
| Private feedback box (admin-only visibility) | Weekly automated data refresh |
| Threshold + deadline → "being heard" notification | |
| Admin dashboard: votes beside district data + export | |
| Scrape 21 Madrid districts (population, council, budget, roadmap) | |

---

## Build approach
- **Built in Lovable.** Web-only, **responsive for mobile and desktop** (QR opens
  the browser — no app store, no install).
- **Backend + DB:** Supabase or Firebase free tier (EU region).
- **No accounts for voters.** Anonymous; one vote per question per device via a
  salted IP+device hash. No personal data collected.
- **The app holds no money** — that's why GoFundMe (Phase 2) stays external.

## Data the creator gets
Per district: yes/no totals and rate, time-to-threshold, share-driven traffic,
private feedback themes, and which districts mobilise fastest — all exportable as
CSV/JSON, shown next to scraped council/budget/population data.

---

## Constraints
- **Timeline:** 1 week. **Budget:** €0 (free tiers only).
- **Platforms:** mobile + desktop web. **Scope:** all 21 Madrid districts.
- **Privacy-first:** anonymous voting, hashed dedupe, EU data, HTTPS, rate-limited.

## Success (end of week)
QR scan → greenery question → yes/no → thank-you + share → threshold fires the
"being heard" notification → admin sees live votes beside district data and can
export. QR codes printed and placed in target districts. GoFundMe and the council
PR story are deferred to Phase 2.
